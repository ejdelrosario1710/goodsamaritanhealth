Introduction: The Scale of the Problem

Using of drugs or illegal substance for early ages has been very popular and a critical problem for the goverment. This problem can be caused by many factors or reasons depending on the mental or physical health of an adolescent. According to the Dangerous Drug Board, “Considering the age at first drug use, forty-two percent (41.96%) belong to ages 15 to 19 years old. Thirty-nine percent (39.04%) admitted to having taken drugs two (2) to five (5) times a week, twenty-six percent (26.27%) monthly, and almost nineteen percent (18.73%) weekly.”
(Source: DDB 2023 Statistical Analysis)

Statement of the Problem: Key Risk Factors

The problem of adolescent drug use is cased by many reasons and factors. Click on each card to learn more about the most common ones.

🤔 Curiosity

An adolescent may be curious to try and answer the question “What’s the feeling” or probably “Nothing will even happen if I try right?”

👥 Peer Pressure

An adolescent may be forced or either wants to fit in a group or circle of friends that is probably using illegal substances, and his way is to join them that is why he might try to do the same.

🏠 Family Problems

An adolescent may be experiencing lack of attention from family members or family members is not treating them fairly, and the mindset would be to use illegal substances or drugs to escape reality.

😔 Depression

An adolelscent may be experiencing self depression which leads him to use drugs to cope up from his feelings and emotions.

Impact on the Developing Brain

The adolescent brain is still under construction, particularly the prefrontal cortex, which governs decision-making and impulse control. Substance use during this critical period can have lasting and severe consequences.

Disrupted Development

Drugs can interfere with the brain's natural "pruning" process, leading to long-term deficits in memory, learning, and executive function.

Increased Addiction Risk

Introducing powerful substances while the brain's reward system is still maturing can "rewire" it for dependency, making addiction more likely and harder to overcome later in life.

🧠

The prefrontal cortex isn't fully mature until the mid-20s. Using drugs before then is like damaging the foundation while the house is still being built.

Short & Long-Term Consequences

The effects of substance use extend far beyond the "high." They impact every aspect of an adolescent's life, from their health to their future. Click each card for details.

💊 Physical & Mental Health

Includes risk of overdose, organ damage (especially to the liver, lungs, and heart), and the worsening of underlying mental health conditions like depression and anxiety, or even triggering psychosis.

🎓 Academic & Social Failure

Substance use is directly linked to poor academic performance, increased absenteeism, and dropping out of school. It can also damage relationships with family and non-using friends.

🔐 Legal & Future Problems

Possession or use of illicit substances can lead to legal trouble, creating a criminal record that hinders future employment and educational opportunities. This creates a cycle that is difficult to escape.

Possible Solution

Effective prevention serves as the primary solution. This requires a comprehensive strategy that addresses risk factors across an adolescent's life.

  • Implement barangay-based programs or seminars that talks about the use of illegal substances and how it can affect your life.
  • Establish a family friendly programs in a community to help family members understand the importance of having one another fairly.
  • Conduct a free therapy for depression and explain how drugs cannot fix depression but rather make it worst.
